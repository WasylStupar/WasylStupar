Caption 1
An internal combustion engine is a type of heat engine in which the fuel mixture is burned directly in the working chamber (inside) of the engine. Combustion products form the working body. Such an engine is primary, chemical, and converts the energy of fuel combustion into mechanical work. There are a large number of different internal combustion engines, differing in purpose, power output, and other parameters.
